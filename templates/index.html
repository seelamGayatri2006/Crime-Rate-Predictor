Crime rate prediction has become a vital tool for law enforcement agencies to understand patterns of crime and anticipate where criminal activity is likely to occur. By predicting future crime trends, agencies can better allocate resources to high-risk areas — leading to a measurable decrease in crime and improved public safety.

The dataset is prepared manually from the Indian National Crime Rate Bureau (NCRB) official website, covering statistics for 19 metropolitan cities from 2014 to 2021 across 10 crime categories including murder, kidnapping, cyber crimes, and crimes against women, children, senior citizens, SC, and ST communities.

This system uses scikit-learn's Random Forest Regression model, an ensemble learning technique that builds thousands of decision trees and averages their outputs for superior accuracy. The model achieves 93.20% accuracy on the test dataset.
